Aliyar Reservoir is a 6.48 km2 (2.5 sq mi) in Aliyar village near Pollachi in Coimbatore District, Tamil Nadu, South India. The dam is located in the foothills of Valparai, in the Anaimalai Hills of the Western Ghats. It is about 65 kilometres (40 mi) from Coimbatore. The dam offers some ideal getaways including a park, garden, aquarium, play area and a mini theme park maintained by Tamil Nadu Fisheries Corporation for visitors' enjoyment. The scenery is beautiful, with mountains surrounding three-quarters of the reservoir. Boating is also available.

History

The Aliyar Dam was constructed from 1959-1969 across the Aliyar River, mainly for irrigation purposes. Aliyar Dam was inaugurated on 2 October 1962 (Gandhi Jayanti day) by then Prime Minister of India, Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru, K Kamaraj, the Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu state presided over the function. The project was commissioned in September 2002 to generate Hydro Electric Power.

The lowest level outlet of the reservoir is 930 feet (280 m) above Mean Sea Level (MSL) and the canal intake is 980 feet (300 m) above MSL. The spillway is 1,040 feet (320 m) above MSL and the FRL (Full Reservoir Level) is 1,050 feet (320 m) above MSL. The maximum surface area is 48 hectares (120 acres) (1,600 acres). The volume of water at FRL is 3.864 Tmcft (88,705 acre-feet (109,416,000 m3)). The maximum depth is 41 metres (135 ft) and the mean depth is 16.8 metres (55 ft). The volume development is 1.2 m. The highest inflow usually occurs during July and August. The shoreline is poorly indented, the shore development is poor and shallow and the Aquatic plants and limnology of the littoral zone is also very limited.

Aliyar Dam is a popular tourist destination. Near Aliyar Dam are some visitor attractions including a park, garden, aquarium, play area and a mini theme park maintained by Tamil Nadu Fisheries Corporation for visitors' enjoyment. The Temple of Consciousness is situated near the Aliyar dam entrance at Arutperunjothi Nagar, where Vethathiri Maharishi resides. The scenery is beautiful, with mountains surrounding three-quarters of the reservoir. Boating is also available. Monkey Falls is located at 6 km from the dam next to Arutperunjothi Nagar and the Forest Department Checkpost. Overnight stay is possible in the forest rest house. A treetop house provides accommodation for the adventurous. The Dam and Park and the overall surroundings of the dam are very poorly maintained as of 2017.
